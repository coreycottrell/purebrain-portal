Context Window

Your AI has a context window — the amount of conversation it can hold in active memory at once. Think of it like a whiteboard: the more you write, the less room remains.

0-60% Green
60-85% Caution
85%+

Green zone — Plenty of room. Your AI remembers everything in this conversation with full clarity.

Yellow zone — Getting full. Your AI may start compressing older parts of the conversation. Consider wrapping up complex topics.

Red zone — Near capacity. Your AI will need to restart soon to continue working effectively. Don't worry — your chat history is saved.

  • Long conversations naturally fill the window — this is normal
  • Your AI's permanent memory persists across all conversations
  • Restarting gives a fresh context with full capacity
  • Code-heavy tasks use more context than simple chat

🦆 The Rubber Duck

When stuck, explain your problem out loud to the duck. The explanation IS the thinking.

Why it works: Articulating a problem forces your brain to linearize it — to pick a starting point, impose sequence, identify what you actually know vs. what you assumed. The act of forming sentences surfaces the contradiction you couldn’t see while staring at it silently.

97% of the time, you solve it mid-sentence. The duck never has to say a word.
